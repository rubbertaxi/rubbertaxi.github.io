Currently reading "A Plea For The Animals".

Matthieu Ricard really left no stone unturned in showing how fallacious we usually are when trying to defend ourselves on the unneccessary, massive killing and suffering of animals. Most of the "reasons" being used boil down to human chauvinism, which is the belief that humans have the right to kill or make animals suffer just because we are humans and everything on earth is to serve our specie; and that as moral rules begin and end at humans, we are excluded from any obligations towards the animals. Struck me surprised since I would believe in the same flow of reasoning not so long ago.
